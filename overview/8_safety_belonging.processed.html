We want your time at QUT to be some of the best years of your life, which is why it's important that you know your rights and responsibilities while you study here. All students and staff at QUT are entitled to a safe and respectful environment.

Our codes of conduct set out the standards of behaviour we expect from our students, staff, and other members of the QUT community.

Disrespectful and problematic behaviour is not tolerated at QUT. This includes:

  • Discrimination
  • Bullying
  • Sexual harassment
  • Sexual assault

Wellness at QUT - We're here to help!

We know that studying at University can be difficult at times, but here in the Faculty of Science and at QUT there are a range of FREE services you can reach out to for help and support.

Get started with HiQ as an initial point of contact for general queries and technology support. Access QUT's free student counselling services.
Report or discuss discrimination, sexual assault or sexual harassment with our Discrimination and Harassment Advisers. Make a free medical appointment at the QUT Medical Centre.
Join the Queer Collective and find out more about Pride at QUT for LGBTIQA+ students and allies. Access a support centre for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander students through the Oodgeroo Unit.
Contact QUT security to organise an escort to your car or a nearby train station. Contact Disability Services for support if you have a short-term or long-term disability, injury or health condition.
Join social events or request an advocate for you in academic proceedings through the QUT Guild. Find out about all the ways QUT can offer financial help here.
